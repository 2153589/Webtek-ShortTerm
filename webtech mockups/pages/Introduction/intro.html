World Wide Web History

- Tim berners lee a british computer scientist who circulates his informational management proposal at the european organization CERN, laying the foundation for the World Wide Web.

- In 1993 Mosaic, the browser credited with popularizing the web was developed by a team led by Marc Andreesen at the University of Illinois. Mosaic was also the basis for the commercial browser in 1994.

- In 1994 China gets the first internet connection but it filters content.

- "David and Jerry's Guide to the World Wide Web" was also launched the forerunner to Yahoo and Amazon.com.

- In 1995, Microsoft released Internet Explorer, which eventually replace Netscape.

- eBay an online auction site was also launched.

- In 1996, Nokia first launch a mobile phone with internet connectivity in Finland.

- In 1998, Google started leading search engine.

- In 2000, the ILOVEYOU internet virus, infects millions of computers around the world.

- In 2001, Napster was ordered to shut down by the US courts in ruling on online copyrights.

- In 2007, Estonia conducts the first online parliamentary election.

- In 2012, online commerce tops $1 trillion, according to private surveys.

- Facebook reaches one billion members a social network.

- In 2013, 2.7 billion or 40 percent of the world's population, people worldwide are connected to the internet.

- Web is a service that operates over the Internet, as e-mail does. Information system on the internet that allows documents and other web resources are identified by UNIFORM RESOURCE LOCATOR (URL), interlinked by hypertext links, and can be accessed via the internet.
